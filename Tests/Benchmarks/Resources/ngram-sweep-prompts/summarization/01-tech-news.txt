> A consortium of major chip manufacturers announced today a new open standard for on-device AI accelerators, aiming to reduce vendor lock-in for developers shipping machine-learning models on consumer hardware. The standard, called NEAT (Neural Edge Acceleration Toolkit), specifies a common runtime API and a shared kernel intermediate representation, allowing the same compiled model to run on accelerators from multiple vendors without recompilation. Initial backers include Apple, Qualcomm, MediaTek, and AMD, with Intel signaling tentative support pending technical review. NVIDIA is conspicuously absent from the launch announcement; analysts speculate that the company's existing CUDA dominance gives it little incentive to join an interoperability effort. The first reference implementations are expected within twelve months, and the consortium has committed to publishing the specification under a permissive open-source license. Developers polled by industry publications expressed cautious optimism, noting that previous cross-vendor standards in the GPU and ML compiler space have struggled to gain traction outside of niche use cases.
